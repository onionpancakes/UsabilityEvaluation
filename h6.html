Recognition Rather than Recall

"Minimize the user's memory load by making objects, actions, and options visible. The user should not have to remember information from one part of the dialogue to another. Instructions for use of the system should be visible or easily retrievable whenever appropriate."

Users are often better are recognizing than recalling information. When designing a webpage, it is important to place high level markers for users to recognize important links for your website.

Indexes on Main Navbar

The University of Leicester website's top global navigation bar consist of only three items: Home, University A-Z index, and maps. Above it, there is a search menu. There is distinct lack of high level menus or groups of links to guide users in navigating the site.
